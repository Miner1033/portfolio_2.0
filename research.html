Healthcare AI & Diabetes Prediction

✓ Published arXiv December 9, 2025

An Improved Ensemble-Based Machine Learning Model with Feature Optimization for Early Diabetes Prediction

Diabetes mellitus is a chronic metabolic disorder affecting millions worldwide, and early prediction remains a critical clinical challenge. This study presents a novel ensemble-based machine learning framework that combines multiple classifiers with advanced feature optimization techniques to achieve superior early-stage diabetes prediction accuracy.

The research leverages the 2015 BRFSS (Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System) dataset — one of the largest health survey datasets in the U.S. — and addresses the significant class imbalance problem inherent in medical datasets using SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique) variants. A rigorous feature selection pipeline was applied to identify the most clinically relevant predictors, reducing dimensionality while preserving predictive power.

The proposed ensemble model outperforms baseline classifiers including Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, and XGBoost in isolation, demonstrating the strength of combining diverse learners with optimized feature subsets for healthcare prediction tasks.
